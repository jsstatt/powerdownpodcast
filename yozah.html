Phonetics variation:

Within the VCV structure the non-fricative consonants usually become palatalized. Outside of this structure they will often fall more towards a standard pronunciation.

Syllable structure:

Nouns most commonly use CVCVC structure while verbs mostly use VCVC. Article vowels are mostly only seen in articles however both phonemes can exist next to each other in other words. Nouns can only end with a vowel. The minimal structure that is allowed is V for articles, CV for nouns and VC for verb.  Consonant cluster can not exceed 2 phonemes without requiring a vowel in between.

Suprasegmental Features:

Tone: Questions are formed using falling tone while statements are made with a medium tone. Aggression can often be expressed with low tone while joy can be expressed with a higher tone.

Stress: The stress of a word usually falls on the nucleus of the syllable so in the common VCV the stress is on the C.

Pronouns:

Unless a pronoun is used on its own, they will always be affixed to the verb. The subject pronoun is attached as a prefix while the object and indirect object pronoun is attached as a suffix.
